Frequntly Asked Questions

What does The pregnancy blood test show?

    "A pregnancy blood test shows the presence and rise of the human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG), a hormone secreted during pregnancy. The hCG nearly doubles every 2 days during early pregnancy, so a measurement by a blood test allows for better understanding of a pregnancy."

What tests should be done during pregnancy?

    "Some of the common tests that you should get done during pregnancy are complete hemogram, thyroid, blood sugar HIV, TORCH tests, HB variance amongst others. Most of these tests are included in the early pregnancy checkup, so you can get a clear understanding of your health and that of your baby."

What tests are done during first trimester of pregnancy?

    "The following tests are generally recommended during the first trimester of pregnancy: Complete hemogram, blood sugar, thyroid, HIV and other STIs, Hb variance, ABO and Rh blood typing among others. Getting a early pregnancy check up is an essential way to determine both the mother and child's health."
